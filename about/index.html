Bāk Institute · About

Who we are &
why we exist

Bāk Institute is an independent, non-profit research organization dedicated to advancing open, equitable, and rigorous science for the Bengali language — one of the world's great literary traditions and one of its most underserved in technology.

The Problem

"Bengali is the 7th most spoken language on Earth, with 230 million native speakers and a literary tradition stretching back a thousand years. Yet NLP benchmarks for Bengali lag entire decades behind English — and most speakers cannot access digital tools in their native tongue."

Our Response

We build infrastructure — corpora, models, tools, and benchmarks — that the global research community can freely use and build upon. We work in the open because Bengali language science should belong to Bengali speakers first.

Our Approach

Rigorous, peer-reviewed research. Inclusive community annotation. Equitable partnership with institutions across Bangladesh, India, and the diaspora. Every output — paper, dataset, model — released under an open license.

Our History

Six years of
open science

2019
Founded in Dhaka
Dr. Minhaj Rahman establishes Bāk Institute with a seed grant from the Open Knowledge Foundation, initially as a team of three researchers focused on corpus construction.
2020
BākoCorpus 1.0 Released
First major output: a 1-billion token Bengali web corpus, freely downloadable. Within six months, it is adopted by research groups at JHU, IIT Bombay, and Edinburgh.
2021
Dialectology Program Launched
Prof. Priya Das joins as Research Director. The Sylheti and Chittagonian documentation fieldwork programs are established, expanding the team to 8 researchers.
2022
ACL Best Paper Award
Bāk receives the ACL 2022 Best Resource Paper Award for the BengaliUD treebank. Dr. Karim Ahmed joins from Google Brain, leading the NLP team expansion.
2023
Fellowship Program & UK Expansion
Launch of the annual Bāk Fellowship for early-career researchers. Partnership with UK diaspora schools to pilot BākoLearn with 240 heritage learners.
2024–25
BākoLM & Multi-Dialect ASR
Release of BākoCorpus 2.0 (5B tokens) and launch of BākoLM pretraining. Bengali↔Tibetan MT at EMNLP. BengaliQA benchmark at ACL 2025.
Our Values

Principles that
guide every decision

01
Radical Openness

Every publication, every dataset, every model weight is released under a permissive open license. No paywalls. No access requests. No NDAs. Ever.

02
Linguistic Equity

We actively invest in underrepresented dialects — Sylheti, Chittagonian, Rajshahi — not just the standard variety. All Bengali speakers deserve language technology.

03
Community Participation

Our annotators are paid fairly. Our partners share authorship. The communities whose language we study are not subjects — they are collaborators.

04
Scientific Rigor

We publish only in peer-reviewed venues. We share negative results. We maintain reproducibility standards across all released code and models.

05
Independence

We accept no funding from sources that would constrain our research agenda, publication rights, or public communication. Our science is not for sale.

06
Long-Term Thinking

Dialect documentation. Treebank construction. Corpus building. These are decade-long commitments. We think in generations, not grant cycles.
