Bengali language research
needs you.

Whether you are a researcher, speaker, developer, annotator, or donor — there is a meaningful place for you in this work.

Ways to Contribute

Five ways to get
involved

Research Collaboration

We partner with universities, research labs, and independent scholars worldwide. Collaborate on datasets, co-author papers, or join a research working group.

Apply to collaborate →
Data Annotation

We recruit volunteer annotators — native speakers of Bengali and its dialects — to help label text, audio, and image data. No technical background required; training provided.

Become an annotator →
Open Source Contribution

All our tools are on GitHub. Contribute code, file issues, review pull requests, or help improve documentation. Every contribution — large or small — matters.

  • GitHub
  • Facebook
Fellowship Program

Our annual fellowship supports early-career researchers — PhD students and postdocs — working on Bengali language technology. Fellows receive a stipend, mentorship, and publication support.

Apply for a fellowship →
Institutional Partnership

We work with schools, NGOs, and diaspora organizations to deploy our tools and literacy programs. If you represent an institution, let's explore what we can build together.

Discuss a partnership →
Donate

Bāk Institute is a registered non-profit. Donations fund fieldwork, server infrastructure, annotator compensation, and fellowship stipends. Every amount makes a difference.

Make a donation →
