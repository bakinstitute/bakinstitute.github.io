Institutions that
believe in open science

We collaborate with universities, research labs, open-source foundations, and community organizations across South Asia, the diaspora, and beyond.

Academic Partners
Core Partner
University of Dhaka
Dhaka, Bangladesh

Home institution of Dr. Rahman and primary anchoring university. Faculty co-appointments, lab space, and HR support for Dhaka-based researchers.

Core Partner
Jadavpur University
Kolkata, India

Longstanding collaboration in Bengali NLP and computational linguistics. Joint laboratory for dialectology research and treebank annotation.

Research Partner
IIT Kharagpur
Kharagpur, India

Collaboration on morphological analysis and parser development. Joint grant funding for the BengaliUD treebank project (2022–24).

Research Partner
University of Toronto
Toronto, Canada

Dr. Karim Ahmed's home institution. Supports Bāk's NLP infrastructure and LLM pretraining research under a research affiliate agreement.

Research Partner
BRAC University
Dhaka, Bangladesh

Joint research programs in EdTech and language learning technology. Hosts Bāk's Dhaka community annotation center.

Research Partner
Presidency University, Kolkata
Kolkata, India

Home institution of Dr. Chakraborty. Joint research in formal Bengali morphosyntax and the BengaliMorph analyzer project.

Funders
Founding Funder
Open Knowledge Foundation
Cambridge, UK

Seed funding in 2019 that made Bāk possible. Ongoing relationship supporting open data infrastructure and policy advocacy for Bengali digital rights.

Grant Funder
Mozilla Technology Fund
San Francisco, USA

2024 grant supporting development of BākoLM — a fully open Bengali language model — as part of Mozilla's Common Voice initiative for language diversity.

EdTech Funder
Silatech
Doha, Qatar

Funds the BākoLearn literacy platform and the UK heritage learner school program, covering development, pilot deployment, and evaluation costs.

Language & Open Source Partners
Language Authority
Bangla Academy
Dhaka, Bangladesh

Official advisory relationship. Bangla Academy provides standard lexical resources, dialect audio archives, and formal guidance on orthographic standards.

Open Data Partner
Wikimedia Foundation
San Francisco, USA

BākoCorpus 2.0 incorporates Bengali Wikipedia content under CC BY-SA. Ongoing partnership to improve Bengali Wikipedia NLP tools and content quality.

Research Partner
AI4Bharat
IIT Madras, India

Collaboration on Indic language NLP infrastructure. Joint corpus data sharing and shared evaluation benchmarks for South Asian languages.
