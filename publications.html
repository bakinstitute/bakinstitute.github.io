BengaliQA: A Large-Scale Open-Domain Question Answering Benchmark
Rahman, M. · Hossain, T. · Chowdhury, S. · Islam, N. · Sen, R. · Ahmed, K. · Das, P.
We introduce BengaliQA, a 50,000-question benchmark for open-domain QA in Bengali, covering 12 domains. We evaluate 8 multilingual models and find significant performance gaps compared to English benchmarks, motivating Bengali-native LLMs.

Tonal Variation in Chittagonian: A Computational Phonological Analysis
Das, P. · Biswas, A.
Chittagonian Bengali exhibits tonal distinctions absent from Standard Bengali. We present the first large-scale acoustic corpus study of 1,200 minimal pairs and develop a computational model of tonal phonology using autosegmental representation.

BākoCorpus 2.0 is a 5-billion token web-crawled corpus for Bengali covering both Bangladesh and West Bengal variants, with document-level metadata, deduplication, quality filtering, and domain tags. Released under CC BY 4.0.

Low-Resource Neural Machine Translation: Bengali↔Tibetan
Chakraborty, M. · Tamang, S. · Islam, F.
We construct a 52,000-sentence Bengali-Tibetan parallel corpus and train transformer NMT models achieving BLEU 22.4 (Ben→Tib) and 19.1 (Tib→Ben), establishing strong baselines for this previously unresourced language pair.

We construct BengaliSentiment, a 25,000-example dataset of Facebook posts annotated for sentiment using code-mixed Bengali, and benchmark 12 multilingual pretrained models. Bengali-focused pretraining provides substantial gains over multilingual baselines.

Sylheti NLP: First Steps Toward Computational Resources for an Under-Resourced Dialect
Islam, F. · Chakraborty, M. · Das, P.
Sylheti has no existing NLP resources. We present the first 500K-word text corpus, a phonemic transcription guide, and baseline POS tagging models achieving 78.2% F1 with limited training data — establishing a foundation for Sylheti language technology.

Transfer Learning for Low-Resource Bengali Automatic Speech Recognition
Sen, R. · Islam, N. · Ahmed, K.
We fine-tune Whisper and wav2vec2.0 on BākoSpeech (10,000 hours), demonstrating that cross-lingual transfer from related languages significantly reduces required Bengali audio. Our best model achieves 8.3% WER — a new state of the art for open Bengali ASR.
