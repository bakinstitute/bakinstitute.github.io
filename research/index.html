Bāk Institute · Research

Six fields.
One language.

We pursue open, rigorous research across six interconnected areas of Bengali language science — from deep learning models to fieldwork in endangered dialects.

Research Areas
অ 01
NLP · AI

Natural Language Processing

We build foundational NLP infrastructure trained natively on Bengali — not adapted from English models. Our work includes large language models, token classification, dependency parsing, and coreference resolution that capture Bengali's unique morphosyntactic properties.

Current Projects

  • BākoLM — Bengali-native large language model (7B parameters)
  • BengaliNER — Named entity recognition across 12 entity types
  • BengaliQA benchmark and evaluation framework
  • Dependency treebank annotation (50,000 sentences)

Tools & Datasets

  • BengaliTokenizer v3 (open-source, pip-installable)
  • BākoCorpus 2.0 — 5B token web corpus
  • BengaliGlue — evaluation benchmark suite
  • POS-tagged corpus (2M words)
View NLP publications →
শ 02
Phonetics · Speech

Speech & Phonology

Bengali's phonological system — with its aspirated stops, inherent vowel patterns, and sandhi rules — presents unique challenges and opportunities for speech technology. We develop ASR, TTS, and acoustic models that capture the full prosodic and tonal range of spoken Bengali across registers and dialects.

Current Projects

  • BengaliASR — 10,000-hour speech recognition corpus
  • Multi-dialect TTS with natural prosody modeling
  • Phoneme inventory documentation for 7 dialects
  • Child language acquisition acoustic study

Tools & Datasets

  • BākoSpeech — 10K hour open ASR corpus
  • BengaliTTS model weights (MIT license)
  • Phoneme-aligned transcription toolkit
  • Forced-alignment tool for Bengali audio
View Speech publications →
গ 03
Computational Linguistics

Morphology & Grammar

Bengali is an agglutinative language with a remarkably complex verb morphology — a single verb form can encode tense, aspect, mood, person, number, and politeness simultaneously. We develop formal computational grammars, morphological analyzers, and syntactic parsers that faithfully represent this complexity.

Current Projects

  • Finite-state morphological analyzer (500K vocab)
  • Honorific scale computational model
  • Bengali Universal Dependencies treebank
  • Verbal paradigm documentation project

Tools & Datasets

  • BengaliMorph analyzer (open-source)
  • Conjugation table generator
  • Bengali UD treebank v2.1 (15K sentences)
  • Lexical database (80K lemmas)
View Morphology publications →
ব 04
Machine Translation

Cross-Lingual Systems

We develop neural machine translation systems for Bengali paired with low-resource languages — a critical gap given that the majority of MT research focuses on English-centric pairs. Our work covers Bengali↔Hindi, Bengali↔Tibetan, Bengali↔Sylheti, and other South and Southeast Asian pairs.

Current Projects

  • Bengali↔Tibetan neural MT (EMNLP 2024)
  • South Asian multilingual translation model
  • Parallel corpus construction for 6 language pairs
  • Domain adaptation for legal and medical text

Tools & Datasets

  • BākoMT model checkpoints
  • Bengali-Hindi parallel corpus (500K pairs)
  • Bengali-Tibetan parallel corpus (50K pairs)
  • MT evaluation benchmark (BengaliBleu)
View Translation publications →
ড 05
Dialectology

Dialect Documentation

Standard Bengali (Shuddhô Bhasha) dominates existing NLP research, leaving millions of speakers of Sylheti, Chittagonian, Rajshahi, Mymensingh, and Rangpur dialects invisible to language technology. We conduct fieldwork, build corpora, and develop computational tools for these underrepresented varieties — several of which face serious endangerment.

Current Projects

  • Sylheti corpus and ASR pilot (3,000 hours)
  • Chittagonian tonal phonology study (JPh 2025)
  • Dialect atlas of Bangladesh (digital, interactive)
  • Rangpur dialect documentation fieldwork

Tools & Datasets

  • Sylheti text corpus (2M words)
  • Dialect word list database (7 varieties)
  • Audio archive of 120 dialect speakers
  • Dialect identification classifier
View Dialectology publications →
শি 06
EdTech · Tools

Language Learning Technology

We design AI-powered tools for heritage learners, diaspora communities, and literacy programs in under-resourced Bengali-medium schools. Our tools include adaptive grammar exercises, an intelligent writing assistant, and a speaking practice companion — all built with learner-centered design principles.

Current Projects

  • BākoLearn — adaptive grammar exercise platform
  • Script learning app for diaspora children
  • Teacher training AI assistant (pilot in 12 schools)
  • Digital literacy curriculum for adult learners

Tools & Datasets

  • BākoLearn app (iOS & Android, free)
  • Graded reader corpus (500 texts, 6 levels)
  • Script recognition model for handwriting
  • Grammar exercise dataset (10K items)
